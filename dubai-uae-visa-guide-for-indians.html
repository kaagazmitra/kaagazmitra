Dubai & UAE Visa for Indian Tourists 2025: The Complete Guide

Published on September 11, 2025

Planning a trip to the dazzling city of Dubai or exploring the wider UAE? From the towering Burj Khalifa to luxurious shopping malls and vast deserts, the UAE is a top destination for Indian travelers. But before you pack your bags, the first step is securing your visa. The process can seem confusing, but with the right information, it's actually quite straightforward.

Eligibility Criteria for Indian Citizens

Before you gather documents, it's important to ensure you meet the basic eligibility criteria. For a UAE tourist visa, an Indian applicant must:

  • Hold a valid Indian passport with at least six months of validity remaining from the intended date of entry into the UAE.
  • Have a clean criminal record and not be on any watchlists.
  • Be in good health.
  • Have a clear purpose of travel, which in this case, is tourism.

Understanding the Types of UAE Tourist Visas

The UAE offers several tourist visa options for Indian citizens, depending on the length and purpose of your stay. Here are the most common ones:

  • 30-Day Single-Entry Tourist Visa: Perfect for a standard holiday. It allows you to stay in the UAE for up to 30 days from the date of entry.
  • 60-Day Single-Entry Tourist Visa: Ideal for a longer vacation or visiting family and friends, allowing a stay of up to 60 days.
  • 30-Day Multiple-Entry Tourist Visa: This visa is useful if you plan to enter and exit the UAE multiple times within a 30-day period (for example, taking a short trip to a neighboring country).
  • 60-Day Multiple-Entry Tourist Visa: Offers the same flexibility as the 30-day multiple-entry visa but for a longer duration of 60 days.

Documents Required for UAE Visa for Indian Citizens

Having your documents in order is the most critical step. Here is a clear checklist of what you'll typically need:

  • Scanned Colour Copy of your Passport: The first and last pages are required. Ensure the scan is clear and all details are readable.
  • Passport-Sized Photograph: A recent, clear photograph with a white background, adhering to the specified dimensions (usually 4.3cm x 5.5cm).
  • Confirmed Flight Tickets: A copy of your round-trip flight tickets from a recognized airline.
  • Proof of Accommodation: Hotel booking confirmation for the duration of your stay or an invitation letter from a resident (friend or family) in the UAE, along with a copy of their Emirates ID and visa page.

How to Apply for a Dubai Tourist Visa

Unlike many other countries, you cannot apply directly at a UAE embassy for a tourist visa. Instead, Indian citizens must apply through a sponsor in the UAE. The easiest ways to do this are:

1. Through Airlines

Airlines like Emirates, Etihad Airways, FlyDubai, and Air Arabia act as sponsors for their passengers. Once you book your flight ticket with them, you can use their online portals to apply for your visa. This is often the most streamlined method.

2. Through Authorized Travel Agents

Common Mistakes to Avoid to Prevent Visa Rejection

To ensure a smooth process, be mindful of these common errors:

  • Poor Quality Scans: Ensure your passport copy and photograph are clear and not blurry.
  • Incorrect Photo Specifications: Submitting a photo with a colored background or an old photograph can lead to rejection.
  • Passport Nearing Expiry: Always double-check that your passport has more than six months of validity.
  • Applying Too Late: While the process is quick, apply at least 1-2 weeks in advance to account for any potential delays.

What to Do After Your Visa is Approved

Once you receive your e-visa, the work isn't over. Take these final steps:

  1. Verify All Details: Carefully check your name, passport number, and visa validity dates on the visa copy. Any error should be reported immediately to your sponsor or agent.
  2. Print a Copy: While it's an e-visa, always carry a clear printed copy with you during your travel.
  3. Understand Validity vs. Stay: Your visa will have a validity period (e.g., 60 days from issue) during which you must enter the UAE, and a duration of stay (e.g., 30 days) which is the maximum number of days you can remain in the country after entry.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Do I need an "Ok to Board" approval for my flight to Dubai?

Previously, "Ok to Board" (OTB) was mandatory for many travelers from South Asian countries. However, it is no longer required for Indian citizens who have a valid visa and are traveling with major airlines like Emirates or Etihad. It's always best to confirm with your specific airline before you fly.

Can I extend my UAE tourist visa?

Yes, tourist visas can usually be extended for an additional 30 days while you are inside the UAE. This process involves a fee and must be done before your current visa expires to avoid overstay fines.

Is travel insurance mandatory for a Dubai visa?

Yes, having valid travel medical insurance that covers COVID-19 is a mandatory requirement for entering the UAE. You may be asked to show proof of your insurance policy upon arrival.
