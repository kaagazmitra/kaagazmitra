Online GST Registration Guide 2025: How to Apply for a GSTIN

Published on August 05, 2025

For any business operating in India, understanding and complying with the Goods and Services Tax (GST) is essential. GST registration is the first step to becoming a legally recognized supplier of goods or services. Obtaining your unique GST Identification Number (GSTIN) not only ensures you are compliant but also allows you to claim tax benefits.

Who Needs GST Registration?

GST registration is mandatory for:

  • Businesses with High Turnover: Any business whose aggregate turnover in a financial year exceeds ₹40 lakhs for goods or ₹20 lakhs for services (with some exceptions for special category states).
  • E-commerce Sellers: Anyone selling goods or services on e-commerce platforms like Amazon or Flipkart, regardless of turnover.
  • Inter-State Suppliers: Businesses making any sale of goods from one state to another.
  • Casual Taxable Persons: Individuals who occasionally supply goods or services in a territory where they don't have a fixed place of business.

The Step-by-Step Online GST Registration Process

The entire process is done online through the official GST portal. Here’s a simplified overview:

Step 1: Generate a Temporary Reference Number (TRN)

Visit the GST portal and fill out Part A of the GST REG-01 form with your PAN, mobile number, and email ID. After OTP verification, you will receive a TRN.

Step 2: Fill Part B of the Application

Using the TRN, log in and fill out Part B of the form. This is where you'll provide details about your business, promoters/directors, authorized signatory, and principal place of business.

Step 3: Upload Required Documents

You will need to upload scanned copies of all necessary documents, such as your PAN card, address proof, and bank details. The full list is provided below.

Step 4: Complete Aadhaar Authentication & Submit

You must verify your application through Aadhaar authentication. Once this is done, you can submit the form. An Application Reference Number (ARN) will be generated and sent to your mobile and email.

Step 5: Verification and Grant of Registration

A tax officer will review your application. If everything is correct, your GST registration will be approved, and your GST Certificate (Form GST REG-06) containing your 15-digit GSTIN will be issued, typically within 3-7 working days.

Documents Required for GST Registration

  • PAN Card of the Business/Applicant.
  • Aadhaar Card of promoters/directors.
  • Proof of business registration (e.g., Certificate of Incorporation).
  • Proof of business address (e.g., electricity bill, rent agreement).
  • Bank account details (a copy of a cancelled cheque or bank statement).
  • Passport-sized photographs of promoters/directors.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How long does it take to get a GSTIN?

If all documents and details are correct, the GSTIN is typically issued within 3 to 7 working days after the submission of the application and generation of the ARN.

Is there a government fee for GST registration?

No, there is no government fee for GST registration. It is completely free. However, professionals charge a fee for their services to ensure the application is filed correctly.

What is the GST Composition Scheme?

The Composition Scheme is a simpler method for small taxpayers to manage GST compliance. Businesses with a turnover of up to ₹1.5 crore can opt for it and pay tax at a fixed, lower rate without needing to file detailed monthly returns.
