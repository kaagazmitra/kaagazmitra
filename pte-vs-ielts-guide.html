PTE vs. IELTS (2025): Which English Test is Right for You?

A complete guide for Indian students to help you choose between the two leading English proficiency exams.

September 18, 2025

If you’re an Indian student dreaming of studying abroad in 2025, you’ll need to prove your English language skills. The two most widely recognized tests are the PTE (Pearson Test of English) Academic and the IELTS (International English Language Testing System). Both are accepted by thousands of universities and governments worldwide, but they are very different exams.

So, which one should you choose? This guide breaks down the key differences between PTE and IELTS to help you make an informed decision that aligns with your strengths and study goals.

PTE vs. IELTS: Key Differences at a Glance

Feature PTE Academic IELTS (Academic)
Test Format Fully computer-based Paper-based and computer-based options
Speaking Test Speaking into a microphone, scored by AI Face-to-face interview with a human examiner
Scoring AI-powered, objective scoring Marked by human examiners
Results Typically within 48 hours 3-5 days (computer) or 13 days (paper)
Duration Approx. 2 hours (single session) Approx. 2 hours 45 mins (Speaking test may be on a different day)

Which Test Format Suits You Best?

PTE: The Fully Digital Experience

The PTE Academic is a single, two-hour test taken entirely on a computer in a secure test center. This includes the speaking section, where you’ll speak into a microphone. Your responses are recorded and graded by an AI algorithm.

Who should choose PTE?

  • Students who are fast typists and comfortable with computers.
  • Those who feel nervous speaking to a person and prefer the anonymity of talking to a computer.
  • Applicants who need their results quickly, as they are often available within two days.

IELTS: The Traditional Approach

IELTS offers both paper-based and computer-based tests for the Listening, Reading, and Writing sections. However, the standout feature of IELTS is its Speaking test, which is a one-on-one conversation with a certified examiner. This feels more like a natural conversation or interview.

Who should choose IELTS?

  • Students who are more comfortable with handwriting or prefer a human interaction for their speaking test.
  • Those who believe their communication skills shine better in a real conversation.
  • Applicants who aren’t in a major rush for their scores.

Scoring and Acceptance

Both tests are scored differently. IELTS uses a 9-band scale (from 1 to 9), while PTE provides a granular score from 10 to 90. Most universities have equivalent score requirements for both tests, so it’s essential to check the specific entry criteria for your desired course and country.

Historically, IELTS has had broader recognition, but PTE is now accepted by thousands of institutions worldwide, including:

  • 100% of universities in Australia and New Zealand.
  • Over 99% of universities in the UK.
  • Thousands of programs in the USA and Canada.

Crucially, both PTE and IELTS are accepted for all visa and immigration applications for Australia and the UK.

The Verdict: Which One Is for You?

There is no "easier" test—the right choice depends entirely on you.

Choose PTE if you are tech-savvy, a fast typist, prefer objective AI-based scoring, and need your results in a hurry.

Choose IELTS if you prefer the option of a paper-based exam, are more confident in a face-to-face speaking interview, and feel a human evaluator will better appreciate the nuances of your communication style.
