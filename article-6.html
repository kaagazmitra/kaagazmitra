How to Update Aadhaar Card Details Online in 2025

Published on July 08, 2025

Keeping your Aadhaar details up-to-date is as important as having the card itself. Fortunately, UIDAI allows you to update information like your name, address, and date of birth online. This guide explains how. If you get stuck, Kaagaz Mitra is here to assist.

Step 1: Log in to the myAadhaar Portal

Visit the official myAadhaar portal (myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in). You will need to log in using your Aadhaar number and the OTP sent to your registered mobile number. This is a crucial step; you cannot proceed without a linked mobile number.

Step 2: Select the Update Service

Once logged in, you will see a dashboard of services. Choose the "Update Aadhaar Online" service. You will then be guided to a new page where you can select which demographic detail you wish to update (e.g., Name, Date of Birth, Gender, Address).

Step 3: Upload Supporting Documents

To update any detail, you must upload a scanned copy of a valid supporting document. For example, to update your address, you can use a recent utility bill, bank statement, or rent agreement. The website provides a list of acceptable documents for each type of update.

Step 4: Make the Payment

There is a nominal fee of ₹50 for each online update request. You can pay this fee using various online payment methods like UPI, net banking, or credit/debit card. After the payment, a Service Request Number (SRN) will be generated, which you can use to track the status of your update request.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What if my mobile number is not registered with Aadhaar?

If your mobile number is not linked, you cannot use the online update service. You must visit a permanent Aadhaar Enrolment Centre to first link your mobile number.

How many times can I update my name on Aadhaar?

According to UIDAI rules, you can update your name on your Aadhaar card only twice in your lifetime. Date of birth can be updated only once.

How long does it take for the details to be updated?

The update process usually takes a few days, but it can take up to 30 days for the changes to reflect. You can track the status using your SRN.
