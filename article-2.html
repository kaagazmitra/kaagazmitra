How to Apply for a PAN Card Online in 2025 – Step-by-Step Guide

Published on July 08, 2025

A Permanent Account Number (PAN) is essential for most financial transactions in India, from filing taxes to opening a bank account. Applying for one is now a simple online process. This guide will walk you through it. If you need assistance, Kaagaz Mitra is here to help.

Phase 1: Application and Payment

Step 1: Choose the Right Portal

You can apply for a PAN card through either the NSDL or UTIITSL portal. Both are authorized by the Government of India. For most individuals, the process is nearly identical on both platforms.

Step 2: Fill Form 49A

On the portal, select the option "New PAN for Indian Citizens (Form 49A)". You will need to fill in your personal details, including your full name, father's name, date of birth, and contact information. Be extremely careful and ensure all details match your Aadhaar card to avoid rejection.

Step 3: Submit and Pay the Fee

After filling the form, you will be prompted to make a payment. The fee is nominal and can be paid online through various methods like net banking, debit/credit card, or UPI. Upon successful payment, a token number will be generated.

Phase 2: Document Submission and Verification

Step 4: e-KYC and e-Sign

The easiest way to complete the process is through Aadhaar-based e-KYC. If you choose this option, your details will be fetched directly from your Aadhaar data. You will then need to e-sign the application using an Aadhaar OTP. With this method, you don't need to send any physical documents.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How long does it take to get a PAN card?

If you apply using Aadhaar e-KYC, you can get the e-PAN card within a few hours. The physical card is usually delivered to your address within 1-2 weeks.

Can I apply for a PAN card without an Aadhaar card?

Yes, you can still apply by submitting physical copies of your documents. However, the process takes longer, and linking PAN with Aadhaar is mandatory.

Is there a fee for a PAN card?

Yes, there is a nominal processing fee of around ₹107 (for Indian addresses), which is subject to change. This fee covers the processing and dispatch of the physical PAN card.
