How to Apply or Update Your Voter ID in 2025

Published on July 08, 2025

Your Voter ID card is a crucial document for exercising your democratic rights and also serves as a valid proof of identity. Whether you're a new voter or need to update your details, the process can be done online. If you find it complicated, Kaagaz Mitra is here to simplify it for you.

For New Voters: Applying for a Voter ID

Step 1: Register on the National Voters' Services Portal (NVSP)

Visit the official NVSP website and register as a new user. You will need a mobile number and email address for verification.

Step 2: Fill Form 6

Once logged in, navigate to the "Register as a New Elector/Voter" section and fill out Form 6. This form requires your personal details, address, and information about your constituency.

Step 3: Upload Documents

You will need to upload scanned copies of your photograph, an age proof document, and an address proof document. Ensure the files are in the specified format and size.

For Existing Voters: Updating Your Voter ID

Step 1: Log in to the NVSP Portal

If you need to make corrections to your name, address, or other details, log in to the NVSP portal.

Step 2: Fill Form 8

Select the option for "Correction of entries in electoral roll" and fill out Form 8. Clearly mention the details that need to be corrected and provide supporting documents for the changes (e.g., a marriage certificate for a name change).

Ensure Your Details are Correct!

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How can I track my application status?

You can track your application status on the NVSP portal using the reference ID you receive after submitting your form.

How long does it take to get a new or updated Voter ID?

The processing time can vary from a few weeks to a couple of months, depending on the verification process by your local electoral office.

Is there a fee for applying or updating a Voter ID?

No, there is no government fee for applying for a new Voter ID or for making corrections to it. The service is free of cost.
